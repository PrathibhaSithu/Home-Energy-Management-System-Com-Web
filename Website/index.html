Literature Survey

Figure 1. Annual coconut production 2011 - 2021 [1]

Household Energy Management Systems (HEMS) have gained increasing attention due to rising energy demands and the push for efficiency in residential settings. Advances in AI, IoT, and renewable energy have enabled smarter solutions for optimizing home utilities. This literature review explores four main domains: solar energy, gas and fire safety, water management, and electricity optimization. AI-based forecasting and hybrid storage systems have shown promise in stabilizing solar power variability and optimizing energy distribution. Yet, gaps remain in predictive maintenance and real-time localized forecasting. Gas leak detection has advanced through MQ-6 sensors and voice-controlled systems, enabling faster emergency response. Deep learning and CNNs have improved fire detection accuracy, replacing basic smoke sensors with real-time visual monitoring.

Water conservation technologies now utilize IoT and AI for leak detection and consumption pattern analysis, though predictive systems are still underdeveloped . Meanwhile, AI-enabled HEMS optimize electricity use through real-time appliance control, demand forecasting, and reinforcement learning. Edge computing and multi-agent models further decentralize control, enhancing scalability and responsiveness. Despite technological progress, challenges remain in cost, interoperability, and standardization, necessitating unified frameworks for fully intelligent, sustainable homes .

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

Existing HEMS often manage electricity, gas, water, and solar energy as isolated subsystems. A unified, AI-driven solution capable of integrating all energy resources into one intelligent platform for centralized control and optimization is lacking.

Severity assessment

Simulation and real-life implementations of the proposed HEMS were performed within a controlled setting. Results prove that this system does indeed optimize energy consumption; provide safety and save other resources in solar energy management, gas and fire detection, water management, and electricity usage.

Comparative Analysis and Implications

Displays marked improvements in response time, predictive accuracy, and real-time adaptability. The joining together of several management modules within a unified system creates a synergistic effect that improves overall household efficiency.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

Current HEMS are fragmented and lack integration across electricity, solar, gas, fire, and water systems. With minimal use of AI for predictive control, they lead to inefficiencies, higher costs, and poor safety automation.

Despite advancements in smart home technologies, existing Home Energy Management Systems (HEMS) remain fragmented, lacking an integrated platform that unifies electricity, solar, gas, fire, and water systems. Most solutions operate in isolation, with limited use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) for predictive control and optimization. As a result, energy inefficiencies, increased operational costs, inadequate safety automation, and minimal user accessibility persist, highlighting the need for a cohesive, intelligent, and adaptive system that proactively manages home energy and safety in real-time.


Proposed Solution

To overcome the limitations of fragmented smart home systems, we propose an integrated Home Energy Management System (HEMS) that unifies electricity, solar, gas, fire safety, and water management. This intelligent platform uses advanced Artificial Intelligence (AI) techniques such as Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for solar energy prediction, Reinforcement Learning (RL) for adaptive energy scheduling, and Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) for decentralized real-time decision-making. The system includes modules for solar optimization, gas and fire safety automation using sensors and deep learning, water usage monitoring and anomaly detection via machine learning, and electricity control using ESP32 microcontrollers and supervised learning. A user-friendly web and mobile interface enables real-time energy tracking, personalized alerts, and voice control, ensuring accessibility for all users. The proposed HEMS enhances energy efficiency, safety, and user engagement while supporting scalability and future integration with technologies like electric vehicles and microgrids, making it a robust solution for modern smart and sustainable homes.

Research Objectives

Towards Sustainable Energy: AI-Based Optimization in Solar Power Systems

This innovative solar energy system integrates AI and advanced hardware to predict energy production accurately, optimize storage, and manage real-time distribution. Using historical data, smart algorithms, and IoT sensors, it enhances efficiency, reduces waste, and boosts system resilience—contributing to a more sustainable and reliable renewable energy infrastructure.

Enhanced Safety Through Intelligent Gas and Fire Management

Safety is a key component of our integrated HEMS. MQ-6 gas sensors and deep learning models for flame detection power our system. In an emergency, it can automatically shut off gas lines and turn off electrical circuits to help prevent accidents. This automated and proactive measure will be extra comfortable to know the household we implemented will be safe, if one of our vulnerable populations - the elderly or children - were in a tampered gas leak or electrical circuit scenario.

Smart Water Usage and Conservation

Water is typically an underappreciated element of energy management. The Home Energy Management System technology includes intelligent water monitoring, including pressure, flow and temperature data. Machine learning models can identify anomalies such as leaks or excessive water usage and notify users in real time. Every drop matters conserving water, saves energy for heating and pumping water too, contributing to the overall management of energy within homes.

Electricity Management System for Sustainable Residential Energy Efficiency

The electricity management module is designed to use ESP32 microcontrollers and environmental sensors to control household appliances based on occupancy, time of use tariffs, and user-defined preferences. Rather than relying on prescribed rules, the electricity management module learns how the user utilizes their appliances and adjusts how it operates over time. The electricity management module schedules the use of appliances intelligently, which reduces unnecessary energy consumption and ultimately saves the user money on their electricity bills while ensuring their comfort is not inhibited.
